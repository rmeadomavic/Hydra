## Round 2 — Counter-Critique

**Confirmed R1-1.** Divergence is the realistic operator failure: they ran Platform Setup once (set [identity]) then hand-edited [tak].callsign during field troubleshooting and expected the latter to stick. The PR's choice (identity wins) is the right default because identity is the cryptographically-tied unit ID, but the dashboard MUST show both so the operator can see what they got. Follow-up: surface both in /api/config/effective and the topbar tooltip. Not a blocker for this PR.

**Challenged R1-2.** Re-read TAKInput._check_duplicate_callsign: the auto-clear only fires when get_status() is called AFTER 60 s of no re-detection. While the duplicate is actively observed (every SA broadcast — 30 s typical interval), the flag stays True and the latch holds. So the one-STATUSTEXT-per-minute worst case requires both (a) sustained duplicate and (b) gaps > 60 s between SA broadcasts AND (c) the facade stats loop running fast enough to catch the rising edge each cycle. In practice the operator sees ONE STATUSTEXT per actual conflict episode, which is correct. Downgrade to nit.

**Confirmed R1-3.** Drift risk is real but follow-up scope — folding facade onto bootstrap is a bigger refactor than fits here.
